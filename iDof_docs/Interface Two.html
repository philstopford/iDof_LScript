About the interface
As with Channels, this plugin has been minimalized to the bare bones necessary to create the effect desired.
 
Of note, this blur works by reading black and white info from the Alpha channel of your render.  If you don’t have iDof Channels outputting it’s Greyscale depth matte to the Alpha channel, you will get incorrect results.
Another tip.  Preview quality renders faster than final, and can save time while setting up the DOF effect that you want.  When you’re about to F10, or send to your renderfarm, put it to final then.
Interface: iDof Post Blur
Select Blur Amount:
   How many pixels to blur your image.  Average settings are between 1 and 7.  Higher settings yield longer render-times.
Select Quality:
   Here you can choose Preview quality or Final quality for the DOF blur.  Preview samples less of the image, so it’s not intended for anything other than visualizing the DOF effect quickly.
 
 
Note:
   A note about the disappeared option to fade the blur.  This option used to smoothly transition from sharp areas to blurred areas based on the greyscale value of the Alpha.  The problem was how the underlying blur worked.  It composited a completely blurred picture using the alpha, so 50% blurred pixels were half blurred, half not (resulting in a less realistic blur).
iDof 3.2 has changed the blur to intelligently increase in amount based on the Alpha value.  No more half blurred pixels.
